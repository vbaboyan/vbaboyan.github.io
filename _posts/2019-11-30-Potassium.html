Potassium and Adrenal Fatigue

A fascinating relationship appears to exist between adrenal function and potassium levels in the body. Studies have shown that cortisol (i.e., the stress hormone) and potassium levels are negatively correlated. Personally, I think a widely neglected factor contributing to such unnatural spikes in cortisol - aside from the lack of adequate vegetable consumption - is excessive caffeine intake [[1]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2257922/). Caffeine has been shown to undesirably stimulate the adrenals, such that levels of cortisol after caffeine consumption are similar to those experienced during an acute stress response. As a grad student, i'll be the first to admit that *I drink wayyyy too much coffee*. Potassium, on the other hand, appears to have a *calming effect* on the body and is even referred to as the body's "natural tranquilizer". In fact, potassium bromide was once used as a sedative for clinical patients and even as an anti-convulsant to treat epilepsy [[2]](https://www.chemistryworld.com/podcasts/potassium-bromide/6805.article). In any event, it's reasonable to expect that the combination of elevated cortisol and depleted potassium can't be good for your body. This combination can potentially lead your body into a chronically active fight-or-flight state, which essentially makes all of your vital organs *hyper-excitable*. Indeed, one of the primary characteristics of an unhealthy adrenal is irritability and anxiety. Once you factor in the elevated cholesterol levels that often accompany the SAD diet, the body's circulation system could be a real mess [[3]](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/full/10.1161/01.CIR.98.12.1198). What are some ways to counteract these mechanisms and get closer to the 4700mg recommendation? Recommendations

One food that isn't represented in this analysis but serves as a fantastic beverage to use in smoothies is coconut water. It typically contains about 500mg of potassium (10% of RDI) per serving (i.e., more than a banana) and tastes delicious. I'd go with any brand that minimizes the added sugars. Every day, I like to blend some coconut water, kale (10%), an avacodo (11%), and a banana (6%) with some ice to make a delcious smoothie to get me going. I also tend to add a scoop of a [plant-based coconut-almond protein powder](https://www.iherb.com/pr/vega-one-all-in-one-shake-coconut-almond-1-5-lbs-687-g/81577) (6%) from wholefoods into the mix and the result is just outstanding. Importantly though, a smoothie like this not only gets you to nearly 50% of the RDI for potassium, but also has so many other nutrients to cover your bases in those areas as well which is great. The rest of your daily needs can easily be satisfied through healthy snacks like chestnuts or brazilnuts, or through a nice side-salad with your meals (e.g.,Kale, Spinach, Taro leaves, Brussel Sprouts).

Summary & Insights

  • Most people don't get enough Potassium due to inadequate consumption of greens.
  • The recommended daily intake (RDI) for Potassium is 4700mg and a single banana has only 300mg.
  • Foods that are high in potassium also tend to be high in Magnesium, Iron, and Fiber. This is what makes vegetables so special.
  • Kale, Spinach, Avocados, and a variety of different nuts are a great source of these vital nutrients.
  • Be cautious of fruits and dates, they are loaded with sugar.
  • A smoothie blend consisting of coconut water, kale, an avocado, and a banana is a great way to get closer to the RDI.
